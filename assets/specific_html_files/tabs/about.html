About Me

Hi! I'm Pablo Turón, an AI Engineer passionate about applying technology to solve real-world problems. I studied Telecommunications and Technology Engineering at the University of Zaragoza, where I discovered my love for using innovation across diverse fields—even enhancing my own performance as a high-level athlete (see the story here).

My engineering thesis marked the beginning of my AI journey through an internship at Sallén Tech. I developed a computer vision system to recognise banknote patterns that achieved the company's accuracy goals in just one-third of the usual computation time, even on low-resource hardware. This sparked my passion for AI applications and led me to pursue an MSc in Artificial Intelligence at the Polytechnic University of Madrid.

During my master's, I entered the Natural Language Processing (NLP) field, working with doctors at Hospital 12 de Octubre to build systems that automatically identified and classified medical entities in clinical records—helping them extract valuable insights with ease.

Since 2020, I have been part of the Vicomtech NLP research team, contributing to both European and private projects. My work spans Information Extraction, Text Classification, Named Entity Recognition (NERC), Document Understanding, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), leveraging both classical techniques and Large Language Models (LLMs). Along the way, I have published research in peer-reviewed journals and shared findings at international conferences (check out my Google Scholar profile for details).
