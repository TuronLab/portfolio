This project was developed as part of the MentalRiskES shared task (IberLEF 2023), which focused on the early detection of mental health risks from Spanish-language Telegram messages. Unlike most prior research that concentrated on English, our work addressed the lack of resources for Spanish, building tools to recognize signals of eating disorders and depression as early as possible.

Our approach introduced a two-phase strategy:

  • Message-level annotation – although the data was labeled at the conversation (stream) level, we developed a method to automatically assign labels to individual messages. This was based on measuring how much each message influenced the model's confidence (Average Confidence Δ).
  • Early risk detection – using this refined dataset, we trained lightweight Transformer-based classifiers (DiagTrast-Berto) capable of predicting disorders from partial message sequences, simulating real-time monitoring.

This methodology allowed our system to focus on the most relevant signals while keeping computational and environmental costs low.

  • Achieved the best official ERDE5 score 1 (0.27) for depression detection and the second-best (0.17) for eating disorders among all participants.
  • Significantly outperformed standard baselines, improving F1-score by up to 15 points using our message-level preprocessing strategy.
  • Built one of the fastest and most resource-efficient systems in the challenge, achieving strong accuracy with minimal carbon footprint.

1. ERDE5 (Early Risk Detection Error at threshold 5) is a metric that measures prediction accuracy while penalizing early or late risk detection within 5% of the ideal prediction time.
